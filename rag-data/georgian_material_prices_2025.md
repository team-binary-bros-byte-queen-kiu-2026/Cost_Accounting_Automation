# Georgian Construction Material Prices — 2025 Reference Guide
Source: Georgian construction market survey (SAMPLE DATA — replace with actual market prices)

## Concrete and Cement Products

Concrete M200 is used for footings, floor slabs, and non-structural elements. Price: 165 GEL/m³.
Concrete M300 is the standard structural concrete for foundations, columns, and beams. Price: 185 GEL/m³.
Concrete M400 high-strength is required for heavy load-bearing structures and earthquake-resistant designs. Price: 210 GEL/m³.
Cement (Portland CEM II 42.5) is sold in 50kg bags. Price: 14 GEL/bag. One m³ of concrete requires approximately 300–400kg of cement.
Ready-mix concrete delivery surcharge is 25 GEL/m³ within Tbilisi. Delivery beyond 30km is negotiated separately.

A typical 120m² two-story house requires approximately 35–45 m³ of concrete total (foundation 15m³, columns/beams 10m³, slabs 15–20m³).

## Masonry Materials

Standard red ceramic brick (250x120x65mm): 0.45 GEL/unit. A 10cm wall requires approximately 52 bricks per m².
Silicate (white) brick: 0.38 GEL/unit. More common for interior partition walls.
Concrete hollow block (400x200x200mm): 1.80 GEL/unit. Popular for exterior walls due to insulation properties.
Aerated concrete (AAC) blocks such as Ytong: 2.50 GEL/unit (600x300x200mm). Provides good thermal insulation.
Pre-mixed masonry mortar (25kg bag): 8.50 GEL. One bag covers approximately 15–20 bricks.

Typical Georgian residential masonry: 40cm thick brick exterior wall costs approximately 95–120 GEL/m² including mortar and labor.

## Steel and Metal

Deformed steel rebar Ø10mm: 1.15 GEL/kg. Standard for floor slabs and light structures.
Deformed steel rebar Ø12mm: 1.20 GEL/kg. Most common size for residential construction.
Deformed steel rebar Ø16mm: 1.25 GEL/kg. Used for columns and heavily loaded beams.
Structural steel I-beam: 1.80 GEL/kg. Used for spanning large openings.
Welded wire mesh 150x150x6mm: 8.50 GEL/m². Used for floor slabs and ground slabs.

Steel reinforcement for a typical foundation: 80–120 kg/m³ of concrete.
Column reinforcement ratio: 1.5–3% of cross-section area by Georgian building codes.

## Roofing Systems

Corrugated galvanized steel roofing: 18 GEL/m². Popular for budget and utility buildings.
Ceramic/clay roof tiles: 32 GEL/m² including underlay. Traditional Georgian appearance.
Metal tile (Metrotile type) with stone coating: 28 GEL/m². Modern look with good durability.
APP bitumen membrane waterproofing: 12 GEL/m². Required under all roof finishes.
Timber roof structure including rafters and purlins: 45 GEL/m² of roof area.

Georgian buildings typically slope roofs at 25–35 degrees. Roof area is typically 1.2–1.4x the floor area for simple gable roofs.

## Insulation and Waterproofing

Mineral (rock) wool 100mm thick: 14 GEL/m². Fire-resistant, used for walls and ceilings.
EPS expanded polystyrene 100mm: 11 GEL/m². Budget insulation for external wall systems.
XPS extruded polystyrene 50mm: 16 GEL/m². Used for foundations and inverted roofs.
Bitumen waterproofing emulsion (2 coats): 9 GEL/m². Applied to foundations and basement walls.
PVC waterproof membrane 1.5mm: 28 GEL/m². For wet rooms, balconies, and flat roofs.

Tbilisi climate requires U-value ≤ 0.40 W/m²K for exterior walls. 100mm mineral wool achieves approximately U=0.38.
