# Georgian Construction Material Prices — 2025 Reference Guide
Source: mymarket.ge — 503 listings scraped from სამშენებლო მასალები category (CatID=2815), June 2026.

## Concrete and Cement Products

Concrete M200 is used for footings, floor slabs, and non-structural elements. Price: 175 GEL/m³ (Tbilisi 2025).
Concrete M300 is the standard structural concrete for foundations, columns, and beams. Price: 195 GEL/m³.
Concrete M400 high-strength is required for heavy load-bearing structures. Price: 220 GEL/m³.
Ready-mix concrete delivery surcharge within Tbilisi: 30 GEL/m³.

Portland cement CEM II 42.5 in 25 kg bags: 16.50 GEL/bag (mymarket.ge median, June 2026).
White decorative cement 50 kg bag: 49.50 GEL (mymarket.ge listing).
One cubic metre of concrete requires approximately 300–350 kg of cement (12–14 bags at 25 kg each).

A typical 120 m² two-storey house requires 35–45 m³ of concrete total: foundation 15 m³, columns/beams 10 m³, slabs 15–20 m³.
At 195 GEL/m³ for M300 plus 30 GEL delivery, the concrete cost for a typical foundation of 15 m³ is approximately 3,375 GEL.
A foundation requiring 18 m³ of M300 concrete costs 18 × 195 = 3,510 GEL in materials.

## Steel and Metal

Deformed steel rebar Ø10 mm (A400/A500): 1.20 GEL/kg. Standard for slabs and light structures.
Deformed steel rebar Ø12 mm (A400/A500): 1.25 GEL/kg. Most common size for residential construction.
Deformed steel rebar Ø16 mm (A400/A500): 1.30 GEL/kg. Used for columns and heavily loaded beams.
Structural steel I-beam: 1.85 GEL/kg. For spanning large openings.
Welded steel mesh 150×150×6 mm: 9.00 GEL/m². Used for floor slabs and ground slabs.

Steel reinforcement for a typical foundation: 80–120 kg/m³ of concrete.
Column reinforcement ratio: 1.5–3% of cross-section area per Georgian seismic building codes.
If steel rebar prices doubled from 1.25 GEL/kg to 2.50 GEL/kg, a 120 m² house would require approximately 8,000 kg of rebar (foundation + columns + slabs), adding roughly 10,000 GEL to the total cost.
