# Construction Specifications and Cost Benchmarks — Georgia 2025
Source: Georgian building practice and construction standards (SAMPLE DATA)

## Typical Cost per m² by Building Type

Budget residential house (brick, basic finishes): 600–900 GEL/m²
Mid-range residential house (brick/block, standard finishes): 900–1,400 GEL/m²
Premium residential house (insulated, quality finishes): 1,400–2,200 GEL/m²
Simple warehouse/industrial shed (metal frame): 350–600 GEL/m²
Commercial office fit-out (existing building): 400–800 GEL/m²
Multi-storey apartment (reinforced concrete frame): 1,200–1,800 GEL/m²

These benchmarks include materials, labor, and site overhead but exclude land, permits, design fees, and VAT.
For budgeting, add 15–25% contingency to the estimated cost.

## Foundation Types

Strip foundation (for loadbearing masonry walls):
- Used for 1–3 story houses on stable soil
- Typical depth: 0.8–1.2m below grade
- Concrete M300, width 400–600mm
- Cost: 180–280 GEL per linear meter (excavation + concrete + steel + formwork + backfill)

Raft/mat foundation (for poor soil or where differential settlement is a risk):
- Covers entire building footprint
- Thickness: 200–400mm
- Requires extensive steel reinforcement (mesh + perimeter beams)
- Cost: 160–260 GEL/m² of floor area

Pile foundation (for very poor or sloped sites):
- Used on unstable soil, steep slopes, or where rock is deep
- Driven or bored piles 300–600mm diameter
- Cost: 1,200–3,500 GEL per pile depending on depth and diameter
- Pile cap and grade beam: additional 200–350 GEL/m²

## Wall Systems

Solid brick (25cm): U-value approximately 1.2 W/m²K. Does not meet modern Georgian thermal standards without additional insulation.
Cavity brick wall (25cm + 100mm EPS + 12cm inner brick): U-value ≈ 0.35 W/m²K. Meets current standards. Total cost approximately 220–320 GEL/m².
AAC block wall (30cm Ytong): U-value ≈ 0.40 W/m²K with rendering. Faster construction than brick. Cost approximately 180–260 GEL/m².
Reinforced concrete frame with brick infill: Most common for 3+ story construction. Frame cost is separate from infill.

## Roof Types for Georgian Climate

Pitched roof (gable, hip): Most suitable for Georgian rainfall patterns. Ceramic or metal tile.
Recommended pitch: minimum 25 degrees for tile, 5 degrees for metal sheet.
Flat roof: Requires careful waterproofing (double membrane). Use XPS insulation above deck.
Green roof: Increasingly popular in Tbilisi. Additional cost 120–200 GEL/m² over standard flat roof.

## Seismic Considerations

Georgia is in a high seismic zone (Zone 3–4 in Tbilisi area, Zone 4–5 in western Georgia).
Georgian building code SP 14.13330 requires earthquake-resistant design.
For masonry buildings, add 10–15% to structural cost for tie beams, ring beams, and columns.
For RC frame buildings, column and beam reinforcement must meet ductility requirements — typically adds 5–8% to structural frame cost.
Soft story irregularity must be avoided — ground floor must not be significantly weaker than upper floors.

## Estimating Guide: Component Quantities per 100 m² Floor Area

For a typical 2-story masonry house (100 m² per floor, total 200 m²):

Foundations:
- Strip foundation: approximately 60–80 linear meters
- Concrete: 12–18 m³
- Steel rebar: 800–1200 kg

Ground floor slab:
- Concrete: 15–20 m³ (150–200mm slab)
- Steel mesh: 100 m²

External walls per floor (perimeter approximately 44m for 10x11m house):
- Wall area: 110–130 m² per floor after deducting openings
- Brick (40cm wall): 11,000–13,500 bricks per floor
- Mortar: 2–3 m³ per floor

Roof (10x11m footprint, gable):
- Roof area: approximately 130–150 m² (factor 1.3–1.5)
- Timber structure: 1.5–2.5 m³ of timber
- Roofing material: 130–150 m²
- Insulation: 100 m² (at ceiling level)

Windows and doors (typical):
- Windows: 15–25 m² total (8–12 windows)
- Exterior doors: 2–3 units
- Interior doors: 6–10 units

## How to Read a Quantity Surveyor's Bill of Quantities

A Bill of Quantities (BOQ) itemizes every material and labor item with quantities and unit prices.
Work packages in a Georgian BOQ typically include:
1. Earthworks and foundations
2. Structural concrete and reinforcement
3. Masonry and blockwork
4. Roofing and waterproofing
5. External wall finishes (render, cladding)
6. Windows and external doors
7. Internal finishes (plaster, tiles, paint)
8. Internal doors and joinery
9. Mechanical (plumbing and heating)
10. Electrical
11. Site work and landscaping
12. Contingency (typically 10–15%)
